Common Questions

FAQ

How much savings do I need to quit a tech job?

Most people should aim for at least 12 months of post-quit runway. If your role is specialized, your household has high fixed costs, or you expect a slow job search, 18 months is safer.

Should I wait for my next RSU vest?

If the vest is close and the amount is meaningful relative to your liquid savings, waiting can improve the decision. If burnout is severe, the vest value may not be worth the cost of staying.

How do I estimate healthcare after quitting?

Start with COBRA or ACA premiums and compare them to what you currently pay through work. Add that difference to your monthly burn before calculating runway.

What is a safe monthly burn after quitting?

A safe burn is one that keeps your runway above 12 months after subtracting severance, one-time costs, and extra healthcare expenses. The calculator shows a target spend cap for that reason.

Is quitting with $100k saved enough?

It can be, depending on your monthly burn, healthcare costs, and whether you have other household income. If your burn is high, $100k may only buy a short runway.

What if I am waiting for a bonus or RSU vest?

Put the value of that vest or bonus into the calculator as unvested equity or severance-like cash, then compare quitting now versus waiting. If the next payout is close, the math may favor delaying.

What should I look at first if my runway is short?

Cut monthly burn first, then re-run the calculator. Small reductions in housing, transportation, travel, or discretionary spending can extend runway more than people expect.
