Burnout vs Financial Readiness: How to Decide

Most bad quit decisions happen when people over-weight one side. If you only look at burnout, you can quit too early. If you only look at money, you can stay too long.

The Two-Layer Test

  • Layer 1: Can you financially survive the exit?
  • Layer 2: Can you mentally and physically survive staying?

If Burnout Is High

If the job is harming sleep, health, or family life, money is not the only variable. In that case, the question becomes whether the financial risk is acceptable enough to protect yourself now.

If Financial Readiness Is High

If your runway is strong, you can give burnout more weight because the financial downside of leaving is smaller. That may make a sabbatical or slower job search reasonable.

Best Practical Rule

If both burnout and runway are bad, reduce spend first and buy time. If burnout is severe and runway is acceptable, leaving may still be the better choice.
