$200k Scenario

Can I Quit With $200k Saved?

$200k is materially more flexible than $100k, but it still depends on burn, household structure, and how quickly you can replace income. It is enough to make quitting plausible for many people, but not enough to ignore the rest of the math.

Quick Rule

At a $7k monthly post-quit burn, $200k can fund a long runway. At a $15k monthly burn, it becomes much less comfortable, especially once healthcare and one-time costs are included.

When $200k Looks Strong

  • Your spending can drop meaningfully after quitting
  • You have some ongoing household income
  • Your RSU opportunity cost is small or already near vest
  • You want a deliberate break, not permanent retirement

When $200k Still Needs Caution

  • High fixed costs
  • Kids, mortgage, or dependent healthcare costs
  • Slow re-employment in your niche
  • Large vesting event in the next few months
Best next step: use the calculator to compare quitting now versus waiting for your next vest or bonus.
