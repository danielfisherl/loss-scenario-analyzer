RSU Tradeoff Guide

Should You Quit Before RSU Vesting?

For many high-income tech workers, quitting is not just a salary decision. It is often a vesting decision. A good call balances financial value with personal sustainability.

The Core Tradeoff

You are weighing immediate freedom against expected vest value.

Wait Value = expected after-tax vest amount - personal cost of staying

If wait value is small and runway is strong, quitting sooner can still be rational.

Estimate Vest Value Realistically

  • Count shares likely to vest in the next event.
  • Apply a downside haircut to current price.
  • Adjust for taxes and withholding.
  • Discount by probability you stay until vest date.

Decision Rules

  • Usually wait: vest is in 1-3 months and runway is under 12 months.
  • Usually flexible: runway is 18+ months and vest value is less than 10% of liquid runway.
  • Usually quit now: staying has meaningful health or performance downside.

Example

Suppose near-term unvested value is $60,000. After taxes it may be around $36,000. If your stay probability is 90%, expected value is about $32,400. Compare that number with your personal cost of waiting.

Practical Scenarios

  • Vest in 1 month: usually wait if the amount is large and your runway is not yet strong.
  • Vest in 3 months: wait only if the value materially changes your decision.
  • Vest in 6+ months: focus more on runway and burnout than the vest itself.

Variables People Miss

  • Reorg or performance risk before vest date.
  • Market risk in the stock price itself.
  • Insurance gap after leaving.
  • Time needed to land an equivalent role.
