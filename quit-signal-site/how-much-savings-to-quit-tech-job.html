How Much Savings Do You Need to Quit a Tech Job?

If you work in tech, the key metric is not salary. It is runway. The practical question is: how many months can you fund your life without forcing a bad next move?

For most high-income tech workers, 12 months is a solid baseline. 18 months is safer when your role is specialized, you have dependents, or your fixed costs are high.

The Rule of Thumb

Required Savings = (Post-quit monthly spend + healthcare increase - other income - break income) x target runway months + one-time costs

The inputs that matter most are your lean monthly spend and healthcare delta after leaving employer coverage.

Runway Targets

  • 9 months: minimum acceptable, higher stress.
  • 12 months: strong baseline for most people.
  • 18 months: best for senior and uncertain hiring environments.

Example Scenarios

  • $200k comp: lean spend $6,500, healthcare delta $900, other income $1,500, one-time costs $5,000. 12-month target is about $76,000.
  • $400k comp: lean spend $10,000, healthcare delta $1,200, other income $2,000, one-time costs $10,000. 12-month target is about $120,400.
  • $800k comp: lean spend $18,000, healthcare delta $1,800, other income $3,000, one-time costs $20,000. 12-month target is about $221,600.

Common Mistakes

  • Using gross salary as a safety proxy instead of monthly burn.
  • Ignoring insurance cost changes after leaving your job.
  • Treating unvested RSUs as guaranteed cash.
  • Planning only one scenario instead of conservative/base/upside.
