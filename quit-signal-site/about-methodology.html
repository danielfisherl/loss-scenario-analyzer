How QuitSignal Estimates Time Off Work

QuitSignal is built for people who want a practical way to compare a temporary break against the real cost of waiting. It is a decision tool first and a calculator second.

What The Calculator Measures

  • How long your savings can support a 3, 6, 12, or 24 month break
  • What happens when you preserve an emergency buffer
  • How monthly living expenses, healthcare, and optional income change the answer
  • Whether the break looks strong, possible but tight, or not yet

Core Assumptions

The calculator uses your liquid savings, one-time inflows, monthly break spending, healthcare costs, expected income during the break, and a reserve target. It also allows a conservative investment return assumption so the result is not unrealistically static.

How The Decision Works

If the target break is covered with room to spare, the signal trends toward strong. If the plan works only with a thin margin, the answer becomes possible but tight. If the target break is not covered once the buffer is preserved, the answer is not yet.

Why This Exists

Most time-off decisions are made with incomplete math. This site tries to make the tradeoff explicit so people can choose with more clarity and less guesswork.

Who This Is For

  • Software engineers, PMs, designers, data and ML professionals
  • People with enough savings to consider a sabbatical or reset
  • Households trying to decide between waiting, taking time off now, or reducing burn first

What It Does Not Do

  • It does not predict your exact future income.
  • It does not replace financial, tax, or legal advice.
  • It does not guarantee an outcome if you take time off work.
Use the calculator to run your own scenario, then compare a 3, 6, 12, or 24 month break against your actual savings and burn.
