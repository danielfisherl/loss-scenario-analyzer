How QuitSignal Works

QuitSignal is built for high-income tech workers who want a fast, practical way to compare quitting now versus waiting. It is a decision tool first and a calculator second.

What The Calculator Measures

  • Cash runway after quitting
  • Current-spend runway if you do not cut costs
  • Healthcare cost delta after leaving employer coverage
  • Near-term RSU or bonus opportunity cost
  • A simple risk label: low, medium, or high

Core Assumptions

The calculator uses your cash savings, severance, one-time quitting costs, monthly spend, other household income, and expected break income. It also checks whether a near-term bonus or vest changes the answer in a meaningful way.

How The Decision Works

If post-quit runway is strong, the signal trends toward quitting. If runway is short, or if healthcare costs and equity tradeoffs are large, the tool shifts toward waiting or reducing spend first.

Why This Exists

Most quit decisions are made with incomplete math. This site tries to make the tradeoff explicit so people can choose with more clarity and less guesswork.

Who This Is For

  • Software engineers, PMs, designers, data and ML professionals
  • People with meaningful equity compensation
  • Households trying to decide between waiting, quitting now, or taking a sabbatical

What It Does Not Do

  • It does not predict your exact future income.
  • It does not replace financial, tax, or legal advice.
  • It does not guarantee an outcome if you quit.
Use the calculator to run your own scenario, then compare quitting now versus waiting for the next vest or bonus.
