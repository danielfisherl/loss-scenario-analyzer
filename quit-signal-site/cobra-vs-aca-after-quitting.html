COBRA vs ACA After Quitting a Tech Job

Healthcare is one of the most common reasons a quit decision goes wrong on paper. The right choice depends on how long you need coverage and how much subsidy you might qualify for.

COBRA

COBRA is usually simple and familiar, but it is often expensive because you pay the full premium. It can make sense if you need continuity or only need a short bridge.

ACA

ACA plans can be cheaper, especially if your post-quit income drops enough to qualify for subsidies. They may take more effort to compare, but the savings can be meaningful.

How To Decide

  • Use COBRA for a short, low-friction bridge when continuity matters.
  • Use ACA when your post-quit income is lower and subsidies are likely.
  • Add the monthly premium difference into your runway calculation.
