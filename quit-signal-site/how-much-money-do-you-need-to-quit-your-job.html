How Much Money Do You Need to Quit Your Job?

The right answer is not a fixed number. It depends on your monthly burn, whether you can reduce spending, how much healthcare will cost after you leave, and whether you expect any income during the break.

The Short Answer

Most people should think in terms of months of runway, not a single savings number. A 12-month runway is a good baseline, while 18 months is safer if your costs are high or your next role may take time.

The Formula

Required savings = (monthly post-quit spend + healthcare increase - other income - break income) x runway target + one-time costs

Plug-And-Play Examples

  • Lean lifestyle: lower spend, some household income, and small healthcare delta can make quitting possible with far less cash than people assume.
  • Middle case: a typical tech household often needs a real cushion because fixed costs do not disappear when the paycheck does.
  • High-burn case: if you are carrying rent, mortgage, childcare, and family coverage, the required number rises fast.
