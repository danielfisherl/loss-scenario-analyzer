Can I Take a Year Off Work in Seattle With $300k?

Decision memo · Updated March 2026 · Built for the time-off-work calculator

A year off in Seattle with $300k is plausible for many high earners, but only if the break is planned as a reduced-burn year rather than a normal spending year with no paycheck.

The answer becomes especially sensitive if you are carrying family costs or expensive housing.

How to think about the year

If your expenses stay moderate, $300k can create real flexibility in Seattle. If your monthly life is expensive, the same balance can still feel tighter than you want for a year that is supposed to create margin.

What changes the answer?

  • Lower housing and predictable healthcare are the biggest win conditions.
  • The year-off plan is stronger if you avoid loading it up with travel and large purchases.
  • A small amount of income during the break can materially improve the result.

Reality check

  • Reality check: a year off should still leave you able to re-enter work without panic.
  • If the buffer at the end of the year is tiny, the plan is thinner than it looks.
