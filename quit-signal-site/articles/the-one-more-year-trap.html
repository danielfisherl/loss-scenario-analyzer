The One More Year Trap

Decision memo · Updated March 2026 · Built for the time-off-work calculator

The one more year trap happens when staying feels rational in the short term, but each extra year quietly becomes the next year. It is one of the most common ways high earners postpone a decision without explicitly choosing to stay.

The trap is not irrational by default. Sometimes one more year really does buy meaningful money or reduce risk. The problem is when the phrase becomes a holding pattern instead of a decision.

How to model it

Compare the value of waiting against the cost of losing another year of freedom, health, and optionality. Then subtract the actual after-tax bonus, vest, or savings increase. If what remains is small, the extra year may be more expensive than it looks.

What changes the answer?

  • The trap gets weaker when you put a date and a dollar amount on the wait.
  • A specific bonus or vest can justify waiting; a vague hope usually cannot.
  • If you would not actually quit after the extra year, you are probably not making a plan yet.

Reality check

  • Reality check: “one more year” often means “I am not ready to decide.” That is a useful diagnosis, but it is not the same as a plan.
  • The fix is to define the exact condition that would cause you to leave, then treat that as a commitment.
