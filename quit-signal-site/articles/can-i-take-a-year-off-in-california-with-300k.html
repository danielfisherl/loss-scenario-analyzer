Can I Take a Year Off Work in California With $300k?

Decision memo · Updated March 2026 · Built for the time-off-work calculator

$300k often gives enough room for a year off in California, but the answer changes a lot with city, housing, and healthcare. A year off is easier when your break is designed around lower burn instead of normal life plus no paycheck.

This is the page for testing whether your plan is merely possible or actually comfortable.

How to read the answer

For a single high earner or a couple with some shared income, $300k can be workable if the monthly burn is managed. For a family with high housing and childcare costs, the same balance may feel much thinner.

What changes the answer?

  • Reducing housing before the break matters more than small investment assumptions.
  • Healthcare costs can move the answer faster than many other variables.
  • If you want travel baked into the break, you need extra room in the budget.

Reality check

  • Reality check: the state does not force the answer; your monthly setup does.
  • If you want the year to feel intentional, not stressful, leave yourself more buffer than the minimum math suggests.
