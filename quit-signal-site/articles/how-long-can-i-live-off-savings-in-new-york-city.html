How Long Can I Live Off Savings in New York City?

Decision memo · Updated March 2026 · Built for the time-off-work calculator

New York City changes the math because fixed costs are heavy and easy to underestimate. Even a strong savings balance can feel less durable when rent, commuting, and healthcare are all part of the same month.

The point of this page is not to pin down an exact cost-of-living number. It is to help you think in ranges and avoid pretending that the same balance works the same way everywhere.

How NYC changes the answer

If you are paying high rent, using private insurance, and keeping an active social life, runway shortens quickly. If your housing cost is shared and your spending can be compressed, NYC stops being automatic death to the plan and becomes a question of discipline.

What changes the answer?

  • Housing is usually the first variable that changes the answer.
  • Transit and city spending can look small individually but add up fast over a long break.
  • A break in NYC is much more sensitive to planning than a break in a lower-cost city.

Reality check

  • Reality check: NYC breaks are not impossible, but they reward precision and punish vague optimism.
  • If you want to stay in the city during the break, model the actual neighborhood, housing setup, and insurance situation instead of a generic number.
