Can I Take 6 Months Off Work With $100k Saved?

Decision memo · Updated March 2026 · Built for the time-off-work calculator

$100k sounds substantial until you map it to six months of real spending. A break that looks obvious on paper can become tight once healthcare and fixed costs are added back in.

For a six-month reset, the useful question is whether the savings create breathing room or just a compressed version of your normal stress.

How to think about it

If your monthly burn after healthcare is near $15k, $100k is not much runway. If your burn is closer to $8k to $10k, the same savings may support a deliberate six-month reset, especially if you can trim spend or bring in some income.

What changes the answer?

  • Your burn rate matters more than your salary did before the break.
  • A 10% cut in monthly spending can be enough to move this from tight to workable.
  • If you need a large emergency buffer, six months becomes harder to justify.

Reality check

  • Reality check: six months is often where people discover whether they planned a break or accidentally planned a slower financial problem.
  • If the goal is recovery plus a thoughtful job search, you want enough room to say no to the first mediocre option.
