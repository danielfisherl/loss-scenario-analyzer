How Much Savings Do I Need to Take Time Off Work?

Decision memo · Updated March 2026 · Built for the time-off-work calculator

The right answer is not a fixed savings number. It depends on how much you spend each month, whether you can lower spending during the break, and whether you need to preserve a real emergency buffer.

For a time-off decision, the best unit is not salary or net worth. It is months of actual freedom.

A simple rule of thumb

Start by estimating your monthly outflow during the break. Multiply that by the length of time you want off, then add healthcare, one-time costs, and a buffer. If the total feels larger than your savings, the break is probably too thin without changes to spending or income.

What changes the answer?

  • The break gets much safer if you can lower spending before starting it.
  • A small amount of monthly income during the break can matter a lot.
  • The emergency buffer should be treated as real money that is not part of the break budget.

Reality check

  • Reality check: people often ask “How much savings do I need?” when the better question is “How much monthly burn can I live with?”
  • The calculator is built to make that translation explicit.
