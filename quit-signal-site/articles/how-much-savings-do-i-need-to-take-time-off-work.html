How Much Savings Do I Need to Take Time Off Work?

Decision memo - Updated March 2026 - Built for the time-off-work calculator

The useful answer is not a single savings number. It depends on how much you spend each month, whether you can lower that spend during the break, and whether you are protecting a buffer on top of the break itself.

For temporary time off, the real unit is not net worth. It is how many months of freedom your cash actually buys.

The simplest way to think about it

Start with monthly living expenses during the break. Add healthcare, add any one-time costs, and then preserve an emergency buffer. If the savings you have left are enough to cover the break length with room to spare, the plan is probably workable. If not, the break may still be possible, but only after lowering burn or adding income.

Three example scenarios

  • If your break costs about $6,000 per month and you want 6 months off, the core spend is already around $36,000 before healthcare or buffer.
  • If your break costs about $10,000 per month and you want 12 months off, the main question is no longer whether savings are large. It is whether your lifestyle can compress without making the break miserable.
  • If you expect even modest consulting or freelance income, the same savings number can support a materially longer break.

What changes the answer?

  • Monthly burn is the biggest lever. Small changes in housing, travel, childcare, or discretionary spending matter more than people expect.
  • Healthcare is often the first hidden cost. It shows up after the paycheck stops, which is why a break can feel more expensive than a quick spreadsheet suggests.
  • A buffer changes the answer because it separates a planned break from a financial emergency. The more serious the break, the more important that distinction becomes.

Common mistake

  • Common mistake: asking whether a savings number is “enough” without first asking what monthly burn that number has to support.
  • Another mistake is assuming the same savings balance means the same thing in every city or household structure.

When this question is the wrong question

If you already know your savings can cover the break, the better question is usually whether you should lower burn first, wait for a payout, or take the time off with a smaller margin. That is the kind of decision the calculator is built to make explicit.
