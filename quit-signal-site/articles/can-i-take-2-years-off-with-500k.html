Can I Take 2 Years Off Work With $500k Saved?

Decision memo · Updated March 2026 · Built for the time-off-work calculator

Two years off is long enough that the decision should be treated carefully. $500k can support it, but only if the plan accounts for healthcare, market swings, and the fact that a two-year break is no longer a short sabbatical.

At this level, the question is not whether the savings look large. It is whether the money can carry you through a long break without forcing a bad job decision at the end.

How to read it

If your spending stays in the low- to mid-single-digit thousands per month and you keep a buffer, $500k can be enough for two years for many households. If you have family costs or a high-cost city lifestyle, the same number loses flexibility quickly.

What changes the answer?

  • The longer the break, the more important conservative assumptions become.
  • Return assumptions matter a little more here, but they should never be the main reason the plan works.
  • If the break includes travel or relocation, the budget needs more headroom.

Reality check

  • Reality check: two-year breaks can quietly become early retirement math if you are not careful.
  • If you want to keep the option to return to work on your own terms, you need to preserve a meaningful buffer all the way through.
