Can I Take a Year Off Work in New York City With $300k?

Decision memo · Updated March 2026 · Built for the time-off-work calculator

A year off in New York City is possible for some households at $300k, but the margin is not automatic. The answer depends heavily on whether your housing cost is controlled and whether you are trying to preserve a true safety buffer.

This is one of those scenarios where the same number can feel either calm or surprisingly tight depending on the shape of your month.

How to read the scenario

If your burn can stay in the low teens or below, $300k can support a one-year break in NYC for many single earners or couples with some shared income. If rent and insurance are high, it may still be more “possible” than “comfortable.”

What changes the answer?

  • Shared housing or lower rent changes the answer more than small spending tweaks.
  • A year off becomes safer if you can keep travel and discretionary spend modest.
  • If you want to return to work without pressure, preserve a larger buffer than you think you need.

Reality check

  • Reality check: the city does not care that you are taking a break. The cost structure stays the same unless you change it.
  • If the plan depends on everything going smoothly, it is probably too thin for a full year.
