Can I Take a Year Off Work With $100k Saved?

Decision memo · Updated March 2026 · Built for the time-off-work calculator

A year off work is a different category of decision. It is long enough that short-term comfort matters less than whether your spending stays disciplined for all twelve months.

$100k can fund a year off only in fairly lean scenarios. For most high earners, the real question is whether a year off should be treated as a controlled sabbatical or as a much tighter financial plan.

The practical read

At roughly $8k per month of total burn, $100k is gone before you include a real buffer. If your life can run closer to $5k to $6k after healthcare, a year off starts to become plausible, but still not generous.

What changes the answer?

  • Long breaks are more sensitive to slow cost creep than short ones.
  • The decision gets much safer if you can reduce housing or household costs before the break.
  • A year off with no income is materially different from a year off with part-time or consulting income.

Reality check

  • Reality check: this is the point where “I can probably make it” is not enough. You want a plan that still works when life gets annoying.
  • If you need a large buffer to feel safe, $100k often becomes too small for a full year.
