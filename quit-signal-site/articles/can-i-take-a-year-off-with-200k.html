Can I Take a Year Off Work With $200k Saved?

Decision memo · Updated March 2026 · Built for the time-off-work calculator

$200k is the first savings level where a full year off starts to look real for many people. It is still not automatic, because the answer changes a lot with rent, family size, and insurance costs.

For analytical users, the key question is whether $200k buys a year of actual margin or just a year of carefully managed stress.

How to read the number

If your post-quit burn is in the high single digits, $200k can support a year off with some buffer left over. If your expenses are high or you need a larger reserve, the same number may still feel uncomfortably thin.

What changes the answer?

  • Household income makes a bigger difference than people expect.
  • A year off is much easier if your monthly spend can drop meaningfully after leaving.
  • If you plan to travel heavily, the answer changes fast.

Reality check

  • Reality check: $200k can be enough without being abundant. That distinction matters if you are trying to avoid a rushed return to work.
  • The right answer is less about whether it is possible and more about whether the month-by-month pressure would be acceptable.
