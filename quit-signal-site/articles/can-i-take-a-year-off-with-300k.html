Can I Take a Year Off Work With $300k Saved?

Decision memo · Updated March 2026 · Built for the time-off-work calculator

$300k creates real flexibility for a one-year break, but the answer still depends on your spending pattern and your tolerance for margin. A year off is easier when the savings cover both the break and a cushion that lets you return on your own terms.

This is the point where the question becomes less about survival and more about how much optionality you want to preserve.

A grounded scenario

If your total burn is moderate and you do not plan to travel aggressively, $300k can support a year off with room left over for a buffer. If you have a mortgage, childcare, or expensive city housing, the same savings can still feel tighter than it first appears.

What changes the answer?

  • The break becomes much safer if you cut spending before you leave.
  • Bonus or severance inflow can push this into the comfortable zone.
  • A one-year break is much more sensitive to healthcare costs than people expect.

Reality check

  • Reality check: $300k is often enough for a year off, but not enough to ignore the structure of the plan.
  • The difference between “possible” and “comfortable” is whether you can finish the year without needing an immediate backup job.
