How Long Can I Live Off $100k Without Income?

Decision memo · Updated March 2026 · Built for the time-off-work calculator

$100k without income is a simple question with a non-simple answer. The number only tells you something useful when it is paired with monthly expenses and healthcare.

A person spending $5k a month is in a very different position than a person spending $12k a month, even though the savings balance is identical.

What the math usually looks like

At around $5k a month, $100k buys a long runway. At around $10k a month, it starts to look like a year or less once healthcare and a small buffer are included. That range is why savings alone is not the decision.

What changes the answer?

  • Cutting monthly burn extends runway faster than almost any other change.
  • Healthcare can meaningfully shift the answer for a household break.
  • A reserve you refuse to touch is not part of the usable runway.

Reality check

  • Reality check: no-income scenarios are really burn-rate scenarios in disguise.
  • If you want a break that feels safe rather than merely possible, you need to model the monthly outflow honestly.
