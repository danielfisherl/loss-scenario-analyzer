The One More Year Trap

The one more year trap happens when staying feels rational in the short term, but each extra year quietly becomes the next year and the next one after that.

How To Model It

Compare the value of staying for one more year against the cost of losing a year of freedom, health, and optionality. Then subtract the real financial upside, like a bonus or vest.

Questions To Ask

  • What exact dollar amount do I gain by waiting?
  • What exact cost do I pay in stress and burnout?
  • Would I actually quit after that one more year?

Common Failure Mode

People often say “one more year” when they really mean “I am afraid to make the decision now.” The fix is to put the numbers into a calculator and set a date.
