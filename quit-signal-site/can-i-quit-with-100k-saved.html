$100k Scenario

Can I Quit With $100k Saved?

$100k can be enough for a quit decision, but only if your monthly burn is modest, healthcare is manageable, and you have some income coming in during the break. The savings number matters less than the runway it actually buys.

Quick Rule

If your post-quit burn is around $5k per month, $100k can buy close to 20 months before taxes, healthcare changes, and one-time costs. If your burn is $10k or more, the runway drops quickly.

When $100k Looks Strong

  • You can cut spend hard after quitting
  • You have spouse or household income
  • You expect consulting or freelance income soon
  • Your healthcare cost increase is limited

When $100k Looks Weak

  • You have a mortgage or childcare costs
  • You rely on a single income
  • You expect a long job search
  • You would lose meaningful near-term RSU value
Best next step: compare your real numbers in the calculator instead of guessing from savings alone.
